1. What information do we collect?

Personal information you disclose to us

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide when you register on the Services or contact us. This may include your name and email address.

Sensitive information — Email content

We access your Gmail inbox via read-only OAuth for the sole purpose of detecting and classifying job-related emails. We use a two-stage process:

  • Stage 1: We analyse only email subject lines, sender names, and snippet previews to determine whether an email is job-related. Non-job emails are discarded immediately and their content is never accessed further.
  • Stage 2: For confirmed job-related emails only, we read the full email body to classify it into one of five categories: Applied, Screening, Interview, Rejected, or Offer. After classification, all email content is immediately discarded from memory and is never stored on our servers.

We store only the extracted metadata: company name, job title, classification status, and date. No email body content, subject lines, or snippets are ever stored in our database.

Information automatically collected

We automatically collect certain information when you visit our Services, including your IP address, browser type, operating system, and usage data. This information is used to maintain the security and operation of our Services.

Google API

Our use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We access Gmail data solely to provide the job tracking service. We do not use Gmail data for advertising, to build user profiles beyond the job tracking purpose, or for any purpose not disclosed in this Privacy Notice.

7. Do we offer artificial intelligence-based products?

Yes. FreeJobTracker.ai uses artificial intelligence to automatically classify job-related emails into five categories: Applied, Screening, Interview, Rejected, and Offer.

Two-stage classification process

  • Stage 1 — Job detection. We analyse only the email subject line, sender name, and snippet preview to determine whether an email is job-related. Non-job emails are discarded immediately and full content is never accessed.
  • Stage 2 — Category classification. Only emails confirmed as job-related in Stage 1 proceed to this stage. We read the full email body and send it to Anthropic's Claude API to classify it. Once the label is returned, all email content is immediately discarded from memory.

What data is sent to our AI provider

For Stage 2 classification, we transmit the following to Anthropic's Claude API:

  • Email subject line
  • Sender name and email address
  • Full email body (for confirmed job-related emails only)

Data retention by our AI provider

Anthropic processes email content solely to return a classification label. Anthropic does not retain or train on this data beyond the scope of completing the API request. See Anthropic's Privacy Policy.

What we store after classification

After classification, we store only: company name, job title, classification status, and date. Email body content, subject lines, and snippets are never written to our database.

How to opt out

You can disconnect your Gmail inbox at any time from your account settings or by visiting myaccount.google.com/permissions. All stored metadata will be deleted within 24 hours of disconnection.
